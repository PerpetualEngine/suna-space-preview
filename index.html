Subject: The tools I wish I'd had at twenty

I was an intensely emotional child, and no one ever taught me what to do with it.

For a long time that meant being run by my own mind. The voice that said I was not good enough, not worthy, too much. If you make things for a living, you have probably met that voice too.

In my mid-twenties I started collecting tools. Meditation that felt impossible at first. Silent retreats. Therapy. Slow, unglamorous practice. One by one, they gave me a way to work with my mind, rather than be dragged behind it.

What surprised me most was how much steadiness came from the hard seasons. Every painful stretch I moved through left me a little more certain that there is nothing that can break me. That kind of trust runs deeper than a mood. Your body earns it by living through things.

If you are in a harder season right now, I want you to know it is doing something. It is quietly showing you how strong you already are.

Just for today, name one hard thing you have already survived. Let yourself feel that you are still standing.

When you want company for the deeper work, my door is open.

A practice

A practice to carry.

Small, simple, yours to keep.

Just for today

Find one quiet moment and let your eyes rest on a single shape. A circle you draw, a candle flame, a symbol you love.

Keep your gaze soft and steady, and let your breath slow on its own. When your attention wanders, and it will, bring it gently back to the shape.

Stay a minute or two. It is one of the simplest tools there is for settling a busy mind, and it asks nothing of you but a little stillness.

On trust, tools, and the long way to your own work

People often ask how I found this work. The honest answer is that I stopped looking for it, and it arrived.

Years ago I let go of most of what I had built. A career in costume and stage design, a relationship, a sense of who I was supposed to be. I went into silence and into a yoga community, and I sat in meditation every morning, which at the time felt close to impossible. One morning I simply started to draw, line by line, thought by thought. That was the first symbol. Within a week, three different people had asked me to tattoo what I had drawn onto their skin. I had never trained as a tattoo artist. I knew, in that moment, that this was the work.

What made it possible was years of slow inner work. I was an intensely emotional child with no tools to handle it, and in my twenties I started gathering them. Meditation, silent retreats, therapy, self-inquiry. None of it was quick and none of it was glamorous, but together they taught me how to be with my own mind rather than be run by it.

The deeper lesson was about trust. To trust yourself and to trust life, I think you have to move through genuinely difficult times and make some peace with pain. The most powerful transformations I know of are the painful ones. Each hard season I survived left me more certain that there is very little that can actually break me, and that I can build something honest and peaceful on the other side.

This is also why pain sits at the centre of what I do. When someone chooses to be tattooed, they choose a little pain on purpose, in exchange for something lasting that means a great deal to them. Pain has always been part of being alive. We are simply out of practice at meeting it, and a great deal of steadiness waits on the other side of learning how.

So when people ask for advice, I keep coming back to the same two words. Trust yourself. Then get quiet enough to hear what you already know, and let life move you from there.

Sacred Geometry Tattoo Meanings: A Handpoke Artist's Guide

If you are drawn to sacred geometry tattoos, it helps to understand what the symbols mean before they become a part of you. Here is a short guide to the ones I am asked for most, and why I choose to place them by hand.

What is a handpoke tattoo?

A handpoke tattoo is made by placing each dot by hand, without a machine. It is quieter and gentler on the skin, and for sacred geometry it lets every line and point be set slowly, with intention.

Flower of Life

Nineteen overlapping circles that have appeared across cultures for thousands of years. It speaks to creation, unity, and the way all living things are woven together.

Seed of Life

The seven circles at the heart of the Flower of Life. It holds the idea of a beginning, of creation finding its very first form.

Metatron's Cube

Drawn from thirteen circles and holding the five Platonic solids, it stands for balance, protection, and the flow of energy through everything that exists.

Vesica Piscis

Two circles meeting and overlapping. The simplest picture of duality becoming unity, of two things together creating a third.

Whatever shape calls to you, the meaning matters as much as the design. If one of these is asking to become yours, I would love to draw it with you.
